Our story

Brewed with
memory &
meaning.

What started as a tiny stall at the Baguio Public Market has grown into a beloved café — but we've never forgotten where we came from, or who taught us how to make the perfect cup.

🫘
Est. 2009
BAGUIO CITY
The beginning

From Lola's
Kitchen

In 2009, Lola Remedios Cruz began selling brewed coffee and fresh pandesal from her home in Burnham Park. Word spread — not through ads, but through neighbors telling neighbors about the cup that tasted like Sunday mornings.

By 2012, we opened our first proper café along Session Road Extension. Lola Remy still visits every Saturday to check on the beans.

Our craft

The Benguet
Difference

We source exclusively from smallhold farms in the Cordillera mountains — communities we've known for over a decade. The cool highland climate and mineral-rich soil produce an arabica with natural brightness and a clean, sweet finish.

We roast in small batches three times a week to ensure every cup is as fresh as possible.

What we believe

Our Values

🌿

Locally Rooted

Every bean, every egg, every loaf comes from within the Cordillera region. We support the farmers who feed our community.

🤝

People First

No rush, no minimums. Pull out a book, catch up with a friend, or just sit with the fog. This place is yours.

☕

Honest Craft

No shortcuts in the kitchen or the roastery. Good coffee takes time, and we think that's worth it.

Founder & Matriarch
